# Vulnversity Journey

When I first opened Vulnversity, I didn't even know what it was or what to do. I struggled to figure out where to test commands, and I even wondered if searching for commands on Google was "right" or if it was considered cheating. Once I discovered where to test them and how to find them, I would run the commands to see if they worked. If they did, I would use **Gemini** and **DeepSeek** to break down the command structure and understand how they were built.

## Task 2

This task was about **Nmap** port scanning and other basics. First, I watched a tutorial on YouTube and then tried to execute the commands on my own. I got stuck on the very first command for about two hours just because I was entering the IP address incorrectly. I thought it was so simple and common that it couldn't possibly be the mistake, so I tried many different tweaks. Eventually, I realized the IP was wrong, and after fixing it, I completed Task 2. I was stuck at the beginning of the questions, but once I got the commands working, the rest of the questions was easy.

## Task 3

Task 3 was about **Gobuster**. I figured out what to do by watching YouTube tutorials, but I couldn't initially understand the logic behind how it worked. I spent an hour deconstructing the Gobuster command structure; it was actually really fun to break the command down. Spending hours trying commands on TryHackMe became frustrating because the "Attack Box" only lasts for one hour. I created two more accounts, but that became a messy and annoying process. Finally, I set up my own **Kali Linux VM**, which is much better than working with the limited time on TryHackMe.

## Task 4

Task 4 was hard at first, but very fun once I started succeeding at the small things. In this task, I learned about **Burp Suite** and **reverse shells**. I studied Burp Suite in detail, learning what the main tools are and how to use them. This task took the most time. I spent 3–4 hours daily for four days straight. Sometimes I felt annoyed and wanted to quit, but I didn't. I kept working, taking 10-minute breaks every hour to think about what I had just learned and what to do next. I spent most of my time finding the logic behind the commands because the tutorials didn't explain them well. Finally figuring it out was incredibly satisfying.

## Task 5

I really enjoyed Tasks 4 and 5 because I finally started to understand how things work and exactly what I was doing. Task 5 was about **Privilege Escalation**, which was new to me. I learned about **SUID** and how to escalate privileges. It was occasionally annoying because I didn't have a formal course to follow; I had to search YouTube, Google, and AI chatbots. As I mentioned before, I learned the most from **Gemini** and **DeepSeek**. Every time I learned something new, the urge to learn even more grew.

And that's how I completed the Vulnversity room. It was a lot of fun!
